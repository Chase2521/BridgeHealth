The Rule of 7's

Double each digit and add half the neighbor. If the digit is odd, also add 5

Here, we will be using the halfing strategy described in the introduction. Remember that odd numbers when halved are rounded down, and half of 1 is 0. Lets work through one example together, then you can work on the practice problems below.

764x7 will be our practice problem. The first step is to remember to mentally put a zero before and after the number.

0 7 6 4 0
Starting with 4 (mentally we immediately think 8), its neighbor is 0. 8 plus half of 0, or 0, is 8. Mentally we would think 8 then 0 (half of 0), then the first number in our answer which is 8.
8
Then we move onto the next digit which is 6. Mentally we should think 12, 2 (half of 4), then 4 which is the next number in our answer. We mentally keep track of the 1 that will be carried over. Remember, we don't mentally keep track of the one, we simply put a dot above the 7.
8 4
Then we move onto 7. Because 7 is odd, we know that we will also add 5 in addition to the number and half its neighbor. Mentally we should think 19 (double 7 and plus 5), then 20 (plus the dot), then 3 (half of six), then 3 which is the next digit in our answer. Now we mentally put two dots over the 0 to carry over the 2 from 22.
3 8 4
Now we are at the last digit in our number which is the 0 that we placed there mentally. In our heads we should think 0 (which is double 0), then 2 (carried over from 7), then 3 (half of seven), and finally 5, which is the last number in our answer.
764 x 7 = 5348
