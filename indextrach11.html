The Rule of 11's

Add each digit to its neighbor

Rather than using long multiplication, when multiplying by 11 each digit is added to its neighbor. You can use small numbers as an example. Take 2 x 11. 2 has two neighbors.

0 <- 2 -> 0
Start by adding 2 to its neighbor on the right, 0, which equals 2. Then add the 0 on the left to its neightbor on the right, which is 2. Thus, your answer is 22. For smaller numbers, this may not be necessary, but it is very helpful when going through problems like the ones below.


Before jumping into more practice adding each of the numbers below to its neighbor on the right. Start from the right, and work your way left.

9 5 6 7 2 3 1
The first few numbers are easier. 1's neighbor is 0, so in your head, you should say "1". When you get to six however, there is another easy trick to help you. Instead of thinking 6 + 7 is 13, just remember the three and mentally place a dot above the 5. When you get to 5, add one to it before adding six. In you head you should see the 5 and think "6 + 6" and remember 2, mentally placing a dot above the 9.
